# Session 9: Make It Actually Useful

[![Open in Colab](https://colab.research.google.com/assets/colab-badge.svg)](https://colab.research.google.com/github/buildLittleWorlds/level-2-course-material/blob/main/session-09/notebook.ipynb)

## What This Session Covers

Students redesign a sentiment analysis demo into a tool for a real audience — a restaurant owner tracking reviews, a teacher reading student feedback, or a therapist monitoring journal entries. They learn how prompt engineering and interface design shape what AI can actually do for people. The concept: Prompt Engineering and Human-AI Interaction. The Big Question: what turns a demo into something someone would actually use?

## Narrative Role

The Act III opener. After eight sessions studying the machine — classification, generation, controls, domain shift, pretraining, bias, and pipelines — students flip the perspective. The question shifts from "how does the model work?" to "who is this for?" This is the most accessible skill in AI: you can't retrain GPT-4, but you can redesign how people interact with it.

## Connections

- **Builds on Session 8** (Chain Two Models Together): Session 8 closed Act II with the full stack — everything inside the machine. Session 9 opens Act III with everything outside it.
- **Bridges to Session 10** (Build Your Own from Scratch): Students have now explored, broken, tuned, tested, chained, and designed. Session 10 is where they build independently.
