# Bluest Hour — AI + Research Level 2 Portfolio

*Simulated exemplar folder for the paper phase.*

## Your Topic

Place-based AI, evening light, reflective journaling, and the question of whether a small mood model can validly read the kind of writing a blue-hour walk creates.

## The new exemplar chain

Open these in order:

1. [Bluest Hour research journal](https://github.com/buildLittleWorlds/bluest-hour/blob/main/research-journal.md)
2. [Bluest Hour simple paper](https://github.com/buildLittleWorlds/bluest-hour/blob/main/PAPER.md)
3. [Bluest Hour source sheet](https://github.com/buildLittleWorlds/bluest-hour/blob/main/SOURCE-SHEET.md)
4. [Bluest Hour almanac paper](https://github.com/buildLittleWorlds/bluest-hour-almanac/blob/main/PAPER.md) only as an advanced continuation

The point of this folder is no longer "copy the fancy paper voice." The point is to show how a project becomes research:

- build the app
- keep a believable journal
- pull out the question, best test, strongest finding, and limitation
- turn those into a short paper

## Where You Are Now

Bluest Hour already has a strong project identity:

- a concrete environmental variable: the blue-hour window
- a place-specific setting: Godfrey, Illinois
- a behavioral recommendation: when to start the walk
- a reflection layer: a short journal note after the walk
- a simple AI layer: a binary sentiment baseline

That is enough to support both a journal and a short paper.

## What To Notice

The journal-first chain makes the research turn visible:

- the first problem is timing
- the second problem is local calibration
- the third problem is whether the journaling model is even measuring the right thing

That last move is the real research move. The AI layer stops being decoration and starts becoming the thing being tested.
