# Bluest Hour — AI + Research Level 2 Portfolio

*Simulated example folder for Session 6 modeling.*

## Your Topic

Place-based AI, evening light, reflective journaling, and the question of whether a small AI tool can meaningfully capture something as subjective as "the bluest hour." The project already has a strong identity: it predicts the best moment for an evening walk, ties that prediction to a real place (Godfrey, IL), and invites a short reflection afterward. That is not a generic student app. It is already a small world with a point of view.

## Where You Are Now

You are farther along than a student who only has an idea. The app exists in two forms: a Hugging Face Space and a GitHub Pages version. You already have a real hook:

- a concrete environmental variable: the blue hour window
- a behavioral recommendation: when to walk
- a reflective layer: a journal entry after the walk
- an AI layer: sentiment analysis on the journal text

That is enough to begin research.

## Live Versions

- **GitHub Pages version:** [buildlittleworlds.github.io/bluest-hour](https://buildlittleworlds.github.io/bluest-hour/)
- **Hugging Face Space:** [hf.co/spaces/profplate/bluest-hour](https://hf.co/spaces/profplate/bluest-hour)

**But here is the honest feedback:** right now the project is still mostly at the *beautiful observation* stage. You can describe why the app feels meaningful. You can point to the Joan Didion quote, the local calibration, the contemplative design. What you have not done yet is ask: **what am I actually investigating?**

The gap is not technical. The gap is analytical. This is exactly the turn Session 6 is designed to help with.

## Where You're Headed

Three deliverables:

1. **A research question** — something narrow enough to test. Not "is this beautiful?" but "what can I find out with this tool that I don't already know?"
2. **Three Spaces that tell a research story** — not just one app duplicated in different places, but a baseline, an experiment, and a fuller version.
3. **A research brief** — a short write-up that says: here's the question, here's the method, here's what I found, and here's what still feels uncertain.
