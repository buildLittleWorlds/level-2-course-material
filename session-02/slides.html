The Punchline

The model can only recognize feelings that someone labeled in the training data.
The binary model was trained on movie reviews.
It only knows thumbs up or thumbs down.
The emotion model was trained on tweets.
It knows 7 emotions because someone decided there are exactly 7 worth labeling.
The zero-shot model wasn't trained on emotions at all.
It matches text to whatever words you give it.

Name the Concept

TRAINING DATA AND REPRESENTATION

same INPUT + different TRAINING DATA → different FEELINGS

"Training data is the textbook the model studied from.
It can only know the feelings it's been shown."

Representation = the categories the data uses.
Positive/negative is one. Seven emotions is another. Your custom labels are a third.

The Big Question

Who decided there are 6 basic emotions?

Ekman's Theory

In the 1960s, Paul Ekman traveled the world showing people photos of facial expressions.

He concluded there are 6 universal emotions:

anger
disgust
fear
happiness
sadness
surprise

The tweet model uses Ekman's list + "neutral." That's where the 7 categories come from.

Ekman studied faces, not words. When someone says "I'm fine" in a flat voice, you know they're not fine — you're reading tone, not text. We'll explore a model that hears voices in a few weeks.

But Is He Right?

Some feelings don't have English words:

Schadenfreude (German) — pleasure at someone else's misfortune
Amae (Japanese) — the sweetness of depending on someone who loves you
Saudade (Portuguese) — deep longing for something absent
Gigil (Filipino) — the urge to squeeze something unbearably cute

If the model was trained on English tweets, what feelings are invisible to it?

The Real Question

If the people who labeled the training data only recognized 7 emotions, the model can only recognize 7 emotions.

What feelings are invisible to it?

Can you think of a feeling you've had that doesn't fit into any of the 7 categories?

The Model Spectrum

Models range from simple to nuanced:

Binary — Positive or Negative
Ternary — Positive, Negative, or Neutral
5-Star — Rates text 1–5 (like a review)
7 Emotions — anger, disgust, fear, joy, neutral, sadness, surprise
28 Emotions — multi-label (admiration, amusement, etc.)

More categories doesn't always mean better. The right model depends on the task.

Vocabulary

Training data — the examples a model learned from
Representation — the categories or labels the training data uses
Sentiment — positive or negative feeling
Emotion categories — the specific feelings a model was trained to recognize
Model card — documentation about what a model does and how it was trained
Fine-tuned — a model further trained on specific data for a specific task
