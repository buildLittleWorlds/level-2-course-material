# Session 10: System Integration — Independent Build
*"Build Your Own from Scratch"*

[![Open in Colab](https://colab.research.google.com/assets/colab-badge.svg)](https://colab.research.google.com/github/buildLittleWorlds/level-2-course-material/blob/main/session-10/notebook.ipynb)

## What This Session Covers

Students choose a model, design a task, and build a complete Hugging Face Space independently for the first time. They work from starter templates and make every decision themselves — what the tool does, who it is for, and how it should look. The concept: Supervised Learning and Task Design. The Big Question: now that you know how these pieces work, what do you want to build?

## Narrative Role

The synthesis. After nine sessions — three acts of exploring, breaking, tuning, and designing — students build something using everything they've learned. Every choice they make embeds a concept from the course: what task (classification vs. generation), what model (training data, labels, supervised learning), what audience (human-AI interaction), what could go wrong (bias, domain shift, error cascades). Session 9's bridge forward set this up: "No templates. No step-by-step guide. Just you and everything you've learned."

## Connections

- **Builds on Session 9** (Make It Actually Useful): Session 9 taught designing for humans — same model, new frame. Session 10 is where students make every decision independently: model, task, audience, interface.
- **Bridges to Session 11** (Iterate and Polish): Session 11 introduces the experimentation loop. Students swap Spaces, get peer feedback, and iterate toward a polished final version.
