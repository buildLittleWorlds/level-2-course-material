Name the Concept

SUPERVISED LEARNING
AND TASK DESIGN
SOMEONE chose the labels
SOMEONE chose the data
SOMEONE chose the task
When you pick a model, you inherit those choices.
That's supervised learning.
That is why your paper can only claim what your model/task choice can honestly show.

Research Lens

You just did end-to-end research integration — connecting a question, a Space result, a verified source, and a limitation.

In a research lab, the tool is not separate from the paper. The tool creates evidence; the paper explains what the evidence can and cannot prove.

Your paper move: "My Space result shows ____; my source helps explain ____; my limitation is ____."

Your Research Journal entry this week documents how the pieces started fitting.
That's research methodology.

Vocabulary

Supervised learning — training with labeled examples where humans provide the correct answers
Task design — deciding what question to ask and what answers are possible
Labels — the categories or answers a model was trained to produce
Model Hub — the Hugging Face library of open-source models, searchable by task
Research prototype — a working tool built to investigate a question
