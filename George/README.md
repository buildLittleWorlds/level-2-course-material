# George — AI + Research Level 2 Portfolio

## Your Topic

Medical and diagnostic AI, with a focus on health communication and explanation. You found this direction organically during the course — you came in interested in music, but something about how AI can explain health concepts grabbed you and pulled you into a different direction entirely. That pivot is real and worth noticing.

## Where You Are Now

You have a working Space: **My_Health_Explainer** is running and live. That's a concrete, significant positive sign. It means you've already crossed from "interested in AI" to "built and deployed an AI tool." But right now your portfolio is mostly that one Space. You don't have a GitHub repo yet. You don't have a research journal. You don't have any writing that articulates *why* this question matters to you or *what* you're actually trying to find out. Those gaps are the most honest thing about where you are — and they're also the easiest thing to close.

## Where You're Headed

Three deliverables:

1. **Three Spaces** — You have Space 1 (My_Health_Explainer). Space 2 should test a specific research question about how different models explain health topics. Space 3 should combine your learnings and push the idea further.
2. **A Research Journal** — Starting now. Not polished, not retroactive. A record of what you're thinking, what you tried, what surprised you, what broke, what you want to try next. The journal is for you first, your readers second.
3. **A Research Brief** — A short write-up (like Prea's) that frames your question, shows what you built, and explains what you learned. This is the artifact that ties everything together.

## What's In This Folder

- **SPACE-PROMPTS.md** — Ready-to-use prompts for building your Spaces. These are customized for health education and already have concrete code examples. Start with Prompt 1 and Prompt 2.
- **RESEARCH-PATH.md** — Your step-by-step roadmap from "I have a Space" to "I have a portfolio." Includes how to sharpen your research question, what to write in your journal first, and how to connect your work to published research on health communication and medical literacy.

## This Week's Priority

**Get on GitHub and start your journal.** The biggest barrier right now is that you don't have a repository. Once you have one, everything else follows. You need:

1. Create a GitHub repo for your portfolio (can be public or private for now).
2. Create a `research-journal.md` file in that repo.
3. Write your first entry — what drew you to medical/health AI? When did the shift happen from music to medical? What are you noticing so far about how AI explains health?

Once you've written that first entry, read RESEARCH-PATH.md. The next section talks about sharpening your research question, and you'll be ready for it because you'll have already articulated where your thinking is now.
