Your paper should come from your actual animation workflow, not from the old emotion-detection entry.

## Best direction

**Recommended research question**

Which AI tools are best at helping with animation scene generation when the real challenge is motion continuity instead of a single pretty frame?

**Good backup question**

What does a tool-switching animation workflow reveal that a one-tool comparison hides?

## Best artifact to write about

Write about **MoodAnimation2** if that is the cleaner version of the project. If `AnimationwithMoods` better shows the first important failure, you can use that instead.

The key is to choose the Space that best shows your actual animation question.

## Pull these things from your journal or notes

- one scene-generation task you gave multiple tools
- what "motion continuity" meant in your own words
- which tools you compared most seriously
- one place where the result felt more like a slideshow than an animation
- one honest limit: thin notes, too many tools, or not enough direct side-by-side testing yet

## Suggested paper map

### 1. What I built

I built an animation-oriented tool or workflow that tries to generate scenes with mood and movement.

### 2. My research question

Your question should come from the difference between a single good frame and a coherent moving scene.

### 3. Why This Matters to Me

This is where you say clearly that animation, not generic emotion detection, is the real center of your interest.

### 4. What I Tried

Use one concrete comparison:

- same animation scene task
- multiple tools
- what you expected
- what each tool did well or badly

### 5. What I Learned

Your strongest likely finding is that motion continuity is a better test than surface quality.

### 6. What Still Needs Work / Who It Might Fail For

Possible limits:

- your notes may still need one stronger analytical entry
- different tools may be good for different parts of the workflow
- animation quality is harder to judge than single-frame quality

### 7. Sources to add or cite

- text-to-video evaluation
- motion continuity / temporal coherence
- animation workflow and storyboard generation

```text
I am interested in animation and AI tools for scene generation.

Help me turn that into one clear research question and one backup question.

Do not invent tests or results.
```
