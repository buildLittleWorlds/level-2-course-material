# Sources: Uncertainty-First Symptom Explainer

These are Consensus-backed candidate sources. They are **abstract-checked only** in this worked example. Before using any source in a real paper, verify the title, authors, venue, DOI if available, and the exact claim the source supports.

- `large language models medical advice uncertainty communication patient facing explanations diagnostic support safety`

## Candidate sources

1. [Current applications and challenges in large language models for patient care: a systematic review](https://consensus.app/papers/details/b963b559a9595606bc9e1161df95b7e6/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   Felix Busch et al., 2025, *Communications Medicine*, citation count: 81.  
   Relevance: Useful because it reviews patient-care LLM uses and maps limitations such as incorrectness, unsafety, bias, and non-reproducibility.

2. [Evaluation and mitigation of the limitations of large language models in clinical decision-making](https://consensus.app/papers/details/4312473ac16b5c21a8f9b7330588bb9e/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   P. Hager et al., 2024, *Nature Medicine*, citation count: 313.  
   Relevance: Useful for the caution section. The abstract reports that current LLMs are not ready for autonomous clinical decision-making in realistic settings.

3. [Large language models encode clinical knowledge](https://consensus.app/papers/details/e576b1acb3465dd4b5bcd1b9787448c7/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   K. Singhal et al., 2022, *Nature*, citation count: 3133.  
   Relevance: Useful because it shows both promise and limitations. The abstract describes medical QA performance but also states that human evaluation reveals gaps.

4. [Large language models in medical and healthcare fields: applications, advances, and challenges](https://consensus.app/papers/details/9e299c4812ff539494320520bc23246c/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   Dandan Wang and Shiqing Zhang, 2024, *Artificial Intelligence Review*, citation count: 78.  
   Relevance: Useful as broad background on applications and challenges such as data security, inaccurate information, fairness, bias, and accountability.

5. [An evaluation framework for clinical use of large language models in patient interaction tasks](https://consensus.app/papers/details/a8a668890e97584f9383d2a06ce16b9b/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   S. Johri et al., 2025, *Nature Medicine*, citation count: 84.  
   Relevance: Useful for method design. It focuses on evaluating LLMs in patient interaction tasks rather than relying only on exam-style benchmarks.

## How these sources would support the paper

- Sources 1 and 4 support the broad claim that healthcare LLMs have promise but also documented risks.
- Sources 2 and 5 support the need for realistic evaluation and careful clinical boundaries.
- Source 3 supports a balanced framing: clinical knowledge can be encoded in LLMs, but safe use requires more than benchmark performance.

## Citation caution

This project should not cite any source as proof that a classroom symptom explainer is medically reliable. A safer claim is: "Medical LLM research shows promise, but repeated reviews emphasize safety, uncertainty, and evaluation limits."
