## Candidate sources

1. [Artificial Intelligence-Assisted Music Education: A Critical Synthesis of Challenges and Opportunities](https://consensus.app/papers/details/3babddcf7375560293f483d2cd97e49a/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   Javier Felix Merchan Sanchez-Jara, Sara Gonzalez Gutierrez, Javier Cruz Rodriguez, and Bohdan Syroyid Syroyid, 2024, *Education Sciences*, citation count: 14.  
   Relevance: Useful for framing AI music tools as learning supports with real pedagogical promise and real limitations. It can support the idea that the project is about music literacy, not just playful generation.

2. [Foundation Models for Music: A Survey](https://consensus.app/papers/details/eceed677debc5dc8b08d9283015e812f/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   Ying-Chao Ma et al., 2024, *arXiv*, citation count: 36.  
   Relevance: Useful background for explaining why music generation is difficult: music has multiple representations, long-range structure, and evaluation problems that are not captured by simple text fluency.

3. [Music for All: Representational Bias and Cross-Cultural Adaptability of Music Generation Models](https://consensus.app/papers/details/a924f24e970b5586ae7bb74f51f3ceb3/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   Atharva Mehta, Shivam Chauhan, Amirbek Djanibekov, Atharva Kulkarni, Gus G. Xia, and Monojit Choudhury, 2025, venue not listed in Consensus output, citation count: 7.  
   Relevance: Useful for the limitation section. If models underrepresent genres or traditions, a genre prompt may produce weaker results for styles less visible in training data.

4. [Analysis of the Music Works by an AI Music Generator and Consideration of Its Music Educational Implications](https://consensus.app/papers/details/9713c25c0adf5e3d9acde1e0cd5e0c96/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   Eunbi Park, 2024, *Korean Music Education Society*, citation count: 0.  
   Relevance: Useful because it evaluates AI-generated music using musical elements such as rhythm, melody, harmony, and form. This points toward scoring outputs by music-specific criteria rather than general pleasantness.

5. [The Use of Deep Learning-Based Intelligent Music Signal Identification and Generation Technology in National Music Teaching](https://consensus.app/papers/details/ed523682c3c2500db9dd2d8ccd603c50/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   Hui Tang, Yiyao Zhang, and Qiuying Zhang, 2022, *Frontiers in Psychology*, citation count: 16.  
   Relevance: Useful as a source direction for genre classification and generation. The abstract reports genre discrimination results, including jazz, classical, rock, country, and disco.

## How these sources would support the paper

- Sources 1 and 4 support the education angle: AI music generation can be useful, but only if students can evaluate what the tool is doing musically.
- Sources 2 and 5 support the technical angle: music genre is not just a word label; it involves representations and measurable musical features.
- Source 3 supports the limitation angle: genre and cultural representation in training data may shape what the model can produce.

## Citation caution

Do not cite a source for a strong claim like "AI understands opera" unless the paper actually studied opera or style understanding. A safer real-paper phrasing would be: "Recent work on music foundation models and music education suggests that genre-aware generation is an active research area, but evaluation remains difficult."
