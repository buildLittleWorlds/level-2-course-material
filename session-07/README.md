## What This Session Covers

Students use their journal, recent notes, and current Space work to produce one of two outcomes:

- a **paper-lite `PAPER.md` draft** if they already have enough material
- a **question + outline artifact** if their journal/build is still too early

The key target is not "write like a literary paper." The key target is:

> show the path from journal to claim

Students should leave the session able to say:

- what they built
- what question grew out of it
- what they tested
- what they learned
- what still needs work or who the tool might fail for

## New Exemplar Chain

The primary Bluest Hour model for this session is now:

1. [Bluest Hour research journal](https://github.com/buildLittleWorlds/bluest-hour/blob/main/research-journal.md)
2. [Bluest Hour simple paper](https://github.com/buildLittleWorlds/bluest-hour/blob/main/PAPER.md)
3. [Bluest Hour almanac paper](https://github.com/buildLittleWorlds/bluest-hour-almanac/blob/main/PAPER.md) as optional advanced reading

The advanced almanac paper stays valuable, but it is no longer the starting target students are asked to imitate.

## Narrative Role

This session is still the pivot from builder to researcher, but the path is clearer now. Students are not being asked to perform a finished "paper voice" on command. They are being asked to organize the evidence they already have.

The fairness and bias theme remains important, but it now lands inside the limitation question:

- who does this tool work less well for?
- what kind of data or writing does the model flatten?
- what part of the tool is honest, and what part still over-claims?

## What Success Looks Like Tonight

### Ready students

Students like Annabelle and Henry should be able to leave with a full paper-lite draft because they already have build evidence and enough journal material.

### Middle students

Students like George should be able to leave with a draft if they can anchor the writing to one real Space and one recent piece of writing.

### Early students

Students like Emily should leave with a sharpened research question, one strong starter outline, and a clear next build step if a full paper would still be fake.

## Core Teaching Move

Do not ask students to "sound academic." Ask them to make the chain visible:

build → journal → question → test → finding → limitation

That chain is the research artifact.
