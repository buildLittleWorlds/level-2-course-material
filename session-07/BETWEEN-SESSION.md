# Between Sessions 7 & 8

This week is about making the draft **more honest and more yours**.

You now have either:

- a paper-lite `PAPER.md` draft
- or a question + outline that shows where the paper is going

Your job this week is not to make it sound fancier. Your job is to make the path from journal to claim clearer.

---

## The three required tasks

### 1. Strengthen your research question

Rewrite the question section so it clearly grows out of something you observed in your journal.

Ask yourself:

- What did I notice?
- What changed in my thinking?
- What exact question came out of that?

If the paper question sounds generic, it is probably too far from the journal.

### 2. Add one real piece of evidence

Your paper needs one concrete test, comparison, or example. Pull this straight from your notes:

- one model comparison
- one output that surprised you
- one prompt grid
- one journal observation
- one limitation that became obvious during testing

Do not replace this with a vague paragraph. Readers trust specifics.

### 3. Write the honest limitation section

This is the most important revision.

Write 1 short paragraph answering:

- what still does not work well
- who might get worse results than you did
- what part of the project is still a guess, a baseline, or unfinished

Bias and fairness live here too. If the model flattens some kinds of writing, works unevenly across users, or is trained on the wrong domain, say so plainly.

## Also do this if you can

- add 2–3 source directions or actual citations
- replace any `[FILL IN]` markers
- tighten the first section so a reader can tell what you built quickly

## If you were not ready for a full paper

That is okay. Your job this week is:

1. keep the question + outline
2. add one more real journal note
3. build or fix the Space that the paper will point to

An honest outline is better than a fake full paper.

## Portfolio check for Session 8

- [ ] `PAPER.md` draft or question + outline is committed
- [ ] the project or Space named in the paper is real and reachable
- [ ] one clear test/example appears in the draft or outline
- [ ] one honest limitation appears in the draft or outline
- [ ] you can explain how the journal led to the paper

---

What matters most is not polish. What matters most is that the draft tells the truth about what you built and what you found.
