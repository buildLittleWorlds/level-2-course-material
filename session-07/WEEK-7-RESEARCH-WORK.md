# Week 7 Research Work

Prereq: your `week-06-research-question.md`.

## This week's work: finding sources with Consensus

Moving from a sharpened question to 3–5 real peer-reviewed papers you could actually cite. The tool is **Consensus** (consensus.app) — an AI-powered academic search engine that returns papers with a one-sentence extract of what each one actually found.

The key move is **search, triage, shortlist**: run a query, read the extracts, click into the ones that look promising, read the abstracts, and build a shortlist. Consensus is a filter, not a library. Every paper that ends up on your shortlist should be one you've at least skimmed the abstract of.

## What to do before Session 8

1. Take the candidate question from your Week 6 file and convert it into a Consensus query. Expect to try 3–5 different phrasings before you get useful results. Log each phrasing and what it returned in a file called `week-07-source-search.md`.
2. Build a shortlist of **3–5 papers** that are actually about your question. For each: title, authors, year; the Consensus extract (copy-paste); URL or DOI; one sentence in your own words about why it's on your shortlist; one sentence about what claim you might eventually cite.
3. Record **near-misses** too — papers that looked promising from the title but turned out to be adjacent. One-line notes. This is your inclusion/exclusion record.
4. End with a one-paragraph reflection: was your question well-supported by the literature? Did you need to reshape it? Reshaping is a win.
5. Bring the file to Session 8.

## Watch out for

- Consensus's yes/no aggregate headers are **not a primary source**. They're summaries across papers. You still have to read the individual papers.
- Paywalled papers — check author websites, Google Scholar "all versions," or arXiv.
- Zero relevant results → your question is either too narrow (widen a dimension) or phrased in language the literature doesn't use (try synonyms).
- 15 papers on a "shortlist" → you skipped the inclusion/exclusion work. Pick the 5 most directly on target.
