# Week 7 Research Work

Prereq: your `week-06-research-question.md`.

## This week's work: finding sources that support your paper

The new paper-lite format still needs sources, but the goal is simple: find 3–5 real papers or source directions that help you explain your question, your method, or your limitation section.

The tool is **Consensus** (`consensus.app`) — an academic search engine that helps you find relevant research papers and read what they actually studied.

The key move is still:

**search → skim → shortlist**

## What to do before Session 8

1. Turn your current research question into 3–5 different search phrasings.
2. Keep a file called `week-07-source-search.md`.
3. For each useful paper, log:
   - title
   - authors
   - year
   - one useful finding from the abstract or Consensus extract
   - one sentence on how it helps your paper
4. Keep 2–3 near-misses too. This helps you show how your question got sharper.
5. Bring the file to Session 8 and use the best 3 items in the "Sources to add or cite" section of your paper.

## A model example: Bluest Hour

The Bluest Hour paper question is:

> When people write short reflections after blue-hour-timed walks, does a generic positive/negative sentiment model actually capture their mood, or does it flatten a more mixed reflective style of writing?

That question breaks into three source buckets:

1. **Nature / daylight / mood**
2. **Reflective writing / journaling**
3. **Sentiment analysis / model fit**

If you want a model for how to do this with your own project, use the same move:

- split the big question into 2–3 smaller source buckets
- search each bucket separately
- write one sentence on how each useful source helps your paper

## Exact Consensus searches you can copy

### Bucket 1 — Nature / daylight / mood

- `nature exposure mood ecological momentary assessment`
- `daylight exposure affect ecological momentary assessment`

### Bucket 2 — Reflective writing / journaling

- `reflective writing well-being review`
- `journaling mood measurement study`

### Bucket 3 — Sentiment analysis / model fit

- `sentiment analysis diary text`
- `emotion classification reflective writing`
- `sentiment analysis health and well-being systematic review`

## Candidate source directions for the Bluest Hour example

These are good examples of the kinds of papers that help the Bluest Hour paper:

- **Beute & de Kort (2018)** — everyday exposure to nature and daylight affecting mood and stress
- **Shankman et al. (2025)** — daylight exposure improving mood independent of physical activity
- **Paredes-Céspedes et al. (2024)** — broad support that nature exposure belongs to a real wellbeing literature
- **Artioli et al. (2021)** — reflective writing as a meaningful method
- **Zunic, Corcoran, & Spasic (2020)** — sentiment analysis in health and well-being is real research, but domain fit is difficult

You do not need these exact papers for your own project. The point is to see how a project question can be connected to a real research neighborhood.

## Watch out for

- Consensus summaries are not the same thing as reading the actual paper.
- If you get zero useful results, your wording may be too narrow or too project-specific.
- If you save 15 papers, you have not shortlisted yet.
