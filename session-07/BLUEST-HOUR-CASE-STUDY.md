# The Bluest Hour Case Study

*A journal-first worked example for Session 7.*

---

## The four stages

| Stage | What it is | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Original project** | The Bluest Hour app in `bluest-hour/` and `bluest-hour-space/` | The build creates the first real question |
| **2. Research journal** | [Bluest Hour research journal](https://github.com/buildLittleWorlds/bluest-hour/blob/main/research-journal.md) | Shows the week-by-week thinking, testing, and limits |
| **3. Simple paper** | [Bluest Hour simple paper](https://github.com/buildLittleWorlds/bluest-hour/blob/main/PAPER.md) | Shows how the paper comes straight from the journal |
| **4. Advanced continuation** | [Bluest Hour almanac paper](https://github.com/buildLittleWorlds/bluest-hour-almanac/blob/main/PAPER.md) | A later, more ambitious redesign and paper voice |

For Session 7, students should copy **Stages 1–3**. Stage 4 is optional and advanced.

## What changed between Stage 1 and Stage 2

The first Bluest Hour project already had a strong point of view, but the journal is where it became research.

The journal shows the actual path:

- sunset was too early
- a centered walk worked better than a single start-time rule
- local observation mattered more than clean astronomy alone
- the journaling layer made the app more interesting
- the binary sentiment model did not fit the writing style the app created

That last move is the key one. The model stops being decoration and becomes the thing being tested.

## What changed between Stage 2 and Stage 3

The simple paper does not invent a new project. It organizes the journal.

This is the exact mapping:

| Paper section | Journal source |
|---|---|
| **What I built** | Weeks 2–3 |
| **My research question** | Week 6 |
| **Why this matters to me** | the motivation across the journal |
| **What I tried** | Weeks 4–5 |
| **What I learned** | Weeks 5–7 |
| **What still needs work / who it might fail for** | Week 7 |

This is the move students should imitate.

## Why Stage 4 is not the first target

The almanac redesign is beautiful and intellectually interesting, but it is a later-stage artifact. It makes sense because the simpler project, the journal, and the initial paper already exist underneath it.

If students start by trying to imitate the advanced paper voice, they skip the part that actually teaches them how research grows. If they start with the journal-first chain, the advanced work becomes a destination instead of a barrier.

## The teaching takeaway

For Session 7, do not say:

> copy the advanced Bluest Hour voice

Say:

> show how your journal turned into a claim

That is the real Bluest Hour lesson.
