# Example Student Portfolio

This folder contains the complete work of **Riley**, a fictional student in AI + Research Level 2. Riley's portfolio is an example of what your work could look like by the end of this course.

Riley is not a real person. The journal entries, Spaces, and portfolio page here were created to show you what the full arc of this course looks like when a student follows through on it — from early exploration to a finished project.

## What's here

- **research-journal.md** — Riley's complete research journal, weeks 1 through 11. Notice how the early entries are short and uncertain, and the later entries get more specific and confident. That progression is normal.
- **README.md** — Riley's portfolio page. This is what a polished GitHub profile README could look like at the end of the course. Yours will look different because your project will be different.

## Riley's arc

Riley got into birding last summer and was curious whether AI could help identify birds from audio recordings. Over the course, Riley moved from generic text generation experiments to a working bird song identification tool — building three Spaces along the way:

1. **Space 1 (Week 5):** A basic text playground using distilgpt2, customized with bird-themed prompts. It generated text about birds but couldn't actually identify anything. Unimpressive but functional.
2. **Space 2 (Weeks 7-8):** A bird sound classifier using a specialized audio model trained on 50 species. Upload a recording, get a species prediction. This was the real turning point — but also a lesson: the best available model only knows tropical birds, not the backyard birds Riley wanted to identify.
3. **Space 3 (Weeks 9-10):** A multi-feature birding assistant that identifies species from audio, shows information about the bird, and lets you log sightings. A pipeline combining audio classification with text generation.

## How to use this example

- **Don't copy Riley's project.** Your domain and interests are different. The point is to see the shape of the work, not the specific content.
- **Notice the journal progression.** Early entries are tentative. Later entries are specific. That's the growth.
- **Notice the Space progression.** Space 1 is intentionally basic. Space 2 is where domain specificity kicks in. Space 3 is where ambition shows up. Your arc should follow the same pattern, even if your topic is completely different.
- **Your portfolio will be yours.** Riley's README is one way to present a portfolio. You'll find your own voice and structure.
