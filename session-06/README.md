# Session 6: Finding Your Research Question
*"What Are You Actually Investigating?"*

[![Open in Colab](https://colab.research.google.com/assets/colab-badge.svg)](https://colab.research.google.com/github/buildLittleWorlds/level-2-course-material/blob/main/session-06/notebook.ipynb)

## Narrative Role

This is the pivot point of the course. Sessions 1–5 were about learning what AI can do — building Spaces, testing models, playing with controls. Session 6 shifts the emphasis: students move from building to investigating. The core activity is formulating a research question, grounded in each student's individual interests and their work so far. The concept of domain — the world a model was trained on — runs through the session as a supporting lens: every research question lives in a domain, and the boundary between a model's domain and a student's domain is where the interesting findings live.

## What This Session Covers

Students open their individual course folders (README.md, RESEARCH-PATH.md, SPACE-PROMPTS.md) and work toward a research question through individual conversations, a question-sharpening exercise, pair discussions, and writing time. The session introduces domain and domain shift as concepts that connect to research question formulation — your topic IS your domain, and testing a model in your domain is the core of your project. The domain shift demos and Colab experiments from the original session design are available for students who want to explore them between sessions or as energy-break interludes.
