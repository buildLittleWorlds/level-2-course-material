The Wall

For years, every model had this problem.
Build a specialized model for every domain.
Expensive. Slow. Never-ending.

Then someone asked:
"What if the training domain was… everything?"

Two Paths Through the Wall

Path A: The Specialist
Fine-tune a small model on your domain.
Small. Fast. Free. Precise.
d4data/biomedical-ner-all
Path B: The Generalist
Pretrain on everything, then fine-tune.
Massive. Expensive. Versatile.
BERT → GPT → ChatGPT / Claude

Both broke through the domain wall. Both are used today.

Research Lens: External Validity

Does your finding hold up outside the original test conditions?

We tested generalization — same method researchers use.

Our research question tonight: How well do generative models work across domains?

Vocabulary

Overfitting — when a model is too specialized in its training data to handle new situations
Domain Shift — when the real-world data is different from the training data
Generalization — a model's ability to work well on data it hasn't seen before
External Validity — whether a finding holds up outside the specific conditions it was tested in
Pretraining — training a model on a massive general dataset before fine-tuning for a specific task
Fine-tuning — retraining a general model on specialized data for a specific task or domain
Named Entity Recognition (NER) — identifying and labeling specific entities (diseases, drugs, people, places) in text
Distribution — the range and patterns of data a model was trained on
