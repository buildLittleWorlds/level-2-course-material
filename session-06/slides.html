The Deeper Question

Language carries feeling differently in different contexts.

A therapist hears "I'm fine" differently than your friend does.

A product review and a love poem exist in completely different domains. The word "great" means something different in each.

Is the model reading the same thing in both?

Name the Concept

MODEL trained on DOMAIN A → works great on DOMAIN A
→ struggles on DOMAIN B → that's DOMAIN SHIFT

Overfitting: the model got SO good at its training data that it can't handle anything else.
Domain shift: the real world is different from the training world.

"Which model is best?" → "Best for what?"

Research Lens

We tested generalization — whether a model works on data it wasn't trained on. In research, this is called external validity.

Does your finding hold up outside the specific conditions you tested it in?

Research question: How well do sentiment models generalize across domains?

In class we applied cross-domain testing to sentiment analysis.
For homework, apply the same method to your own topic.

Vocabulary

Overfitting — when a model is too specialized in its training data to handle new situations
Domain shift — when the real-world data is different from the training data
Generalization — a model's ability to work well on data it hasn't seen before
Domain — a category of text (tweets, legal documents, poetry, product reviews, etc.)
External validity — whether a finding holds up outside the specific conditions it was tested in
