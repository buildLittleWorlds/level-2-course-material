These are Consensus-backed candidate sources. They are **abstract-checked only** in this worked example. Before using any source in a real paper, verify the title, authors, venue, DOI if available, and the exact claim the source supports.

## Candidate sources

1. [Political Bias of Large Language Models in Few-Shot News Summarization](https://consensus.app/papers/details/4f82187cb0de5b5eb8c272c135e312bf/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   Takeshi Onishi and James Caverlee, 2024, venue not listed in Consensus output, citation count: 1.  
   Relevance: Very close to the project. It studies political bias in news summarization and notes potential word-choice bias.

2. [Bias of AI-generated content: an examination of news produced by large language models](https://consensus.app/papers/details/0f7771ac19cf5827af0542a601a11172/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   Xiao Fang, Shangkun Che, Minjia Mao, Hongzhe Zhang, Ming Zhao, and Xiaohang Zhao, 2023, *Scientific Reports*, citation count: 121.  
   Relevance: Useful background on bias in AI-generated news-like content.

3. [Quantifying Generative Media Bias with a Corpus of Real-world and Generated News Articles](https://consensus.app/papers/details/86da550116e6521fbf5f10a9304fa3a9/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   Filip Trhlik and Pontus Stenetorp, 2024, venue not listed in Consensus output, citation count: 10.  
   Relevance: Useful because it compares human-authored and generated news articles and focuses on political bias.

4. [Decoding News Narratives: A Critical Analysis of Large Language Models in Framing Bias Detection](https://consensus.app/papers/details/15885d296c655d6cb30d50bbc2feefea/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   Valeria Pastorino, Jasivan Sivakumar, and N. Moosavi, 2024, *arXiv*, citation count: 3.  
   Relevance: Useful for defining framing bias and for discussing how hard it is for models to distinguish framing from emotional language.

5. [Bias in News Summarization: Measures, Pitfalls and Corpora](https://consensus.app/papers/details/f0555e874ac85a0da6d7cbd3578072bc/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   Julius Steen and Katja Markert, 2023, venue not listed in Consensus output, citation count: 11.  
   Relevance: Useful for the method section because it focuses on measuring bias in summarization and warns about evaluation pitfalls.

## How these sources would support the paper

- Sources 1, 3, and 5 support the summarization-specific framing.
- Sources 2 and 4 support the broader concern that generated news can contain bias or framing shifts.
- Together, they support an ethical question: should a news tool claim neutrality, or should it show perspective explicitly?

## Citation caution

Do not cite these sources as proof that a particular classroom Space is politically biased. A safer claim is: "Research on LLM news generation and summarization shows that framing and bias are measurable concerns."
