# Parallel Example: Model UN News Lens

This packet models how an interest in politics, international relations, and ethics could become a Hugging Face Space research project. It is adjacent to a news-summarization idea, but it does not describe the student's actual work.

## Core idea

The imagined Space is a **Model UN News Lens**. A user pastes the same news article or headline into a tool and chooses a diplomatic perspective. The Space generates summaries from several stated perspectives and highlights what changed.

## Research question

When an AI tool summarizes political news from different stated perspectives, how much does the framing actually change?

## Space idea

Inputs:

- News headline or short article excerpt
- Perspective: neutral briefing, humanitarian NGO, national-security adviser, climate delegate, opposition party, or youth delegate
- Summary length

Outputs:

- Perspective-specific summary
- Key words emphasized
- What was included, softened, or left out
- Reflection prompt asking whether the summary is balanced, biased, or useful for debate preparation

## What this packet demonstrates

- How to make political framing visible without pretending perfect neutrality exists
- How to connect Model UN-style thinking to AI summarization
- How to cite research on summarization and media bias cautiously
- How to write an early-stage paper even before a full Space exists
