## Where You're Headed

Three deliverables:

1. **Three Spaces** — each more ambitious than the last, exploring your research question about how AI handles political news and information. The first one is simple (a baseline). The second tests something specific. The third is your best work.
2. **A research journal** — weekly entries tracking what you've tested, what surprised you, what you've learned
3. **A research brief** — a 3–4 page write-up with a real research question, related work from peer-reviewed sources, methods, results, and what you'd do next

This is exactly what Prea built. You can see her work at `../example-student-prea/`. She came in with debate experience (like you have Model UN experience). She built a working tool, ran an experiment, wrote it up, and cited real papers. You can too.

You're doing two things in parallel from now on: **building Spaces** and **writing about what you're building and why**. For you, the writing side needs to start immediately because you don't have a journal yet — but building should be happening at the same time. Here's the plan:

1. **Write your first journal entry this week** — what you've noticed about AI and news/politics so far, what interests you, what you want to explore. See RESEARCH-PATH.md for the framework.
2. **Sharpen a research question** — RESEARCH-PATH.md gives you three candidate questions at different levels of specificity. Pick the one that feels most promising.
3. **Start building your first Space** — Use the prompts in SPACE-PROMPTS.md. Your first Space doesn't have to be impressive — it's a baseline. Paste one of the prompts into Claude or ChatGPT and get something deployed. Your second and third Spaces will be more ambitious.

The writing and the building inform each other. What you notice while building becomes journal material. What you write about in your journal shapes what you build next.
