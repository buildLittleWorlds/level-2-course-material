## Part 1: Hub Challenge — Test the Mood Meter with Text You Care About

In class we explored a sentiment analysis Space that assigns emotional labels and confidence scores to text. Before we learn formal research methods starting in Session 2, this week is about building a habit that will matter all semester: **noticing when a model's reading matches yours and when it doesn't**.

1. Go to [profplate/mood-meter](https://huggingface.co/spaces/profplate/mood-meter) (or any Space from your Collection)
2. Feed it text that matters to you:
   - A lyric from a song you've been listening to
   - A paragraph from a book you're reading
   - A text you sent or received recently (change the names if you want)
   - Something you'd write in a journal
3. For each piece of text, write down: **(a)** what the model said, **(b)** what you felt, and **(c)** whether you agree with the model's reading
4. Try at least **5 different inputs** — aim for variety (different emotions, different lengths, different kinds of text)

### What to Notice

- Does the model agree with how YOU felt reading the text?
- When the model says POSITIVE — do you feel positive about it too?
- When it says 97% confident — does that change how you trust the result?
- Can you find text where the model is clearly wrong about the feeling?
- Do you notice any patterns in what the model gets right vs. wrong?

### Write It Down

Keep notes on what you tested and what happened. You don't need a formal format yet — just capture enough detail that you could describe your findings next session. Starting in Session 2, we'll formalize this into a Research Journal, but for now, just get in the habit of recording what you observe.

### Bring It Back

Next session, be ready to share: what text did you try, what did the model say, and did you agree?

## Part 2: Grow Your Collection — Start Exploring What Interests YOU

Add at least **2 more Spaces** to your Collection before next session. Your target by Session 2 is **2 models and 3 Spaces** total.

For each Space you add, write a **tasting note** using the Space Explorer's Field Guide questions:

1. What goes in?
2. What comes out?
3. What model powers it?
4. What happens with weird input?

These tasting notes are the start of a documentation practice you'll use all semester. In later weeks, you'll add more detail — what the model was trained on, where it fails, how it compares to others — but for now, these four questions give you a foundation.

**Important:** Your Collection is yours. In class, we'll use sentiment analysis as our shared example to learn research methods together. But your Collection should follow YOUR curiosity. Sentiment and emotion Spaces are great — but so are image classifiers, translation tools, music generators, code assistants, or anything else that catches your eye. Over the next 11 weeks, your Collection will grow into a portfolio of everything you've explored and investigated.

Browse [huggingface.co/spaces](https://huggingface.co/spaces) to find Spaces to try. Search for "sentiment" or "emotion" if you want to stay close to tonight's topic, or search for anything that interests you.
