The Wall

Classification models choose from a fixed menu.

3 labels… 7 labels… 28 labels…
More labels ≠ more understanding.

It's still just picking from a list someone else created.

Thought Experiment: Spam Detector

You build a spam detector. You test it on 1,000 emails.

950 are real mail. 50 are spam.
The model predicts "NOT SPAM" for every single email — all 1,000.
What's the accuracy?

The Accuracy Trap

95% accurate!

It got 950 out of 1,000 correct.

But it never catches any spam.

This is a classification model. It sorts into two buckets.
And even at 95% accuracy, it's useless. That's the wall.

False Positives vs. False Negatives

False Positive
Real email marked as spam
Annoying — you miss a real email
False Negative
Spam that gets through
Could be dangerous — phishing, fraud
Which is worse? It depends on the task.

The Fork in the Road

Classification Generation
What it does Sorts input into categories Creates new content
Output One label from a fixed menu A sequence of new words
Example "great movie" → POSITIVE "Once upon a time" → "there was…"

The Training Data Difference

Classification

Needs labeled data.
A human decided every label.
"This tweet is positive."
"This email is spam."

124 million tweets, each labeled by a human.
Generation

Needs just text.
No labels. No human decisions.
The model reads and predicts
the next word.

8 million web pages. No labeling required.

The Insight That Changed Everything

Labels are expensive.
Someone must decide each one.

Text is everywhere.
The entire internet is training data.

When you don't need labels, you can train on everything.

That's exactly what happened. And that's how we got ChatGPT, Claude, and every generative AI tool.

Name the Concept

CLASSIFICATION VS. GENERATION

Classification: sort into buckets → fixed menu → needs labels
Generation: create new content → next-word prediction → needs just text

Everything in Sessions 1–3 was classification.
Everything from here forward traces what happened
when people chose generation.

Vocabulary

Classification — AI that sorts inputs into categories from a fixed menu
Generation — AI that creates new content by predicting the next word
Labeled data — training data where a human decided the correct answer for each example
Next-word prediction — how generation models learn: read, predict, check, adjust
Parameters — internal numbers a model adjusts during training (distilgpt2: 82M, GPT-4: 1T+)
