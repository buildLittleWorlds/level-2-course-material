## What This Session Covers

This session is the fork in the road — the transition from Act I (The Old Way) to Act II (The Breakthrough). Students replay the story of Sessions 1–3 (classification, training data, adversarial testing) and hit the wall: classification models don't understand, they just sort into buckets. Then they see a completely different kind of AI — a text generation model — for the first time. The concept: Classification vs. Generation. The question: what if AI didn't just sort things, but could create something new?

The session includes the spam detector thought experiment (demonstrating why accuracy can be misleading and introducing false positives/negatives), a live demo with distilgpt2 using the adversarial stories from Session 3, and the core teaching about training data: classification needs labeled data (expensive), generation needs just text (everywhere). That difference is what made scale — and generative AI — possible.
